# KAUTILYA IAS 60-Card Diagnosis Extension

## Decision

Preserve every existing diagnosis card and its wording. Extend the current 50-card premium instrument to 60 cards, and expand the curated free instrument from 30 to 40 cards.

All diagnosis cards remain multiple choice. Reports must derive only what the selected options support and must never invent names, exact relationships, scores, ranks, posts, motives, or personal history.

## Product Principles

1. Preserve the existing psychological voice, pacing, and visual card treatment.
2. Add evidence only where the current instrument cannot reliably support the new report outputs.
3. Keep the existing eight-level journey. Do not introduce a ninth level.
4. Treat the report as interpretation, not fortune-telling.
5. Give free users a complete foundational profile. Premium adds specificity, contradiction analysis, and a deeper operating charter.
6. Keep existing 30-card and 50-card reports readable after launch.

## New Cards

The final copy may receive punctuation-level editing during implementation, but its meaning and option architecture should remain stable.

### L2-07 - Target Post

**Question:** When you picture the work after selection, which chair are you actually preparing to earn?

**Options:**

- IAS: field administration, policy, and executive responsibility.
- IPS: command, public order, and institutional leadership.
- IFS: diplomacy, negotiation, and representing India abroad.
- Revenue or economic services: taxation, finance, trade, or regulation.
- Another Group A service whose work fits me better than its prestige.
- I want selection first; I have not chosen a service honestly yet.

**Purpose:** Records a service/post family without pretending to know an exact cadre or posting.

### L2-08 - Target Rank

**Question:** Strip away polite modesty. What rank band is the campaign in your head built to pursue?

**Options:**

- Top 10. I am preparing for first-choice certainty.
- Top 50. My target assumes a very high service allocation.
- Top 100. I want a strong chance at a preferred service.
- A rank that secures my chosen service, whatever that number becomes.
- Any place in the final list would change the war.
- I have never converted the dream into a rank target.

**Purpose:** Distinguishes ambition, service-led calibration, selection urgency, and unquantified aspiration.

### L2-09 - Target Score

**Question:** Which numerical line is most real in your preparation plan right now?

**Options:**

- A repeatable Prelims GS score with a clear safety margin above recent cutoffs.
- A CSAT score that makes qualification boring rather than frightening.
- A Mains written score strong enough that the interview is not a rescue mission.
- A final aggregate benchmark tied to my preferred service and rank.
- I track improvement and safety margin, not one permanent number.
- No score line is written down yet.

**Purpose:** Captures the aspirant's active score target without hard-coding cutoff-sensitive numbers.

### L6-08 - Emotional Vault Trigger

**Question:** Which thought has the most reliable key to the room you do not show people?

**Options:**

- "What if the years amount to nothing?"
- "What if I disappoint the people who carried me?"
- "What if others move ahead and I remain here?"
- "What if I am financially dependent for too long?"
- "What if failure proves I was never as capable as I believed?"
- "What if nobody really understands what this preparation is costing me?"

**Purpose:** Identifies the primary emotional vault without asking for unsafe disclosure or free text.

### L7-06 - Human Anchor Relationship

**Question:** When the fight becomes private, whose presence has the strongest claim on your courage?

**Options:**

- My mother.
- My father.
- My parents or family as a whole; separating one person would be false.
- My partner, sibling, or closest friend.
- A teacher, mentor, or senior who saw something in me.
- No single person. My strongest anchor is not human.

**Purpose:** Identifies the relationship category while preserving privacy.

### L7-07 - Human Anchor Role

**Question:** What does that person or circle actually do inside your preparation?

**Options:**

- Their sacrifice turns quitting into a debt I refuse to leave unpaid.
- They believed before there was evidence, and I protect that belief.
- They calm my nervous system when the exam makes everything feel unstable.
- They hold me accountable and do not let my excuses become a lifestyle.
- They represent the life and security I want to build after selection.
- They are part of a memory or legacy I carry forward.
- My anchor is not a person, so none of these is the closest truth.

**Purpose:** Separates relationship from emotional function so the report can form supported composite anchors.

### L7-08 - Character Anchor

**Question:** When you need to remember the kind of person this war is meant to build, which figure do you reach for?

**Options:**

- A constitutional public servant known for courage and administrative integrity.
- A reformer or freedom fighter who accepted a long struggle.
- A strategist, thinker, or historical leader whose discipline steadies me.
- A family elder whose character matters more to me than public fame.
- A mentor, senior, or selected aspirant whose conduct feels attainable.
- No borrowed figure. I work best from my own principles.

**Purpose:** Supports a character anchor without forcing celebrity worship or inventing a named role model.

### L7-09 - Deep Motivator

**Question:** If applause, comparison, and family expectations went silent for one year, what would still make you return to the desk?

**Options:**

- The work itself: I want responsibility over problems that matter.
- Restoration: I want to change what is possible for my family.
- Proof: I need to know what my full capacity can do.
- Freedom: the career would give me agency, security, and a larger life.
- Mastery: becoming capable enough for this exam has meaning of its own.
- Recognition: I want the authority and standing that selection brings.
- A promise: something unfinished would remain unfinished if I walked away.

**Purpose:** Distinguishes intrinsic, relational, identity, security, mastery, status, and vow-based motivation.

### L8-06 - Personal Law

**Question:** Which law, obeyed for the next 90 days, would protect you from your most predictable self-sabotage?

**Options:**

- One final source per subject; repetition outranks collection.
- A daily minimum survives even when the ideal schedule collapses.
- Testing and retrieval happen before I feel perfectly prepared.
- Every week ends with evidence, review, and one corrected command.
- A bad result earns analysis and recovery, never disappearance.
- Plans are written from available hours and energy, not fantasy.
- I know my weakness, but I have not yet chosen the law that governs it.

**Purpose:** Supplies one explicit candidate law. Final reports may derive additional laws only from corroborating answers.

### L8-07 - Operating Rhythm

**Question:** Under real pressure, which operating rhythm produces your best work most consistently?

**Options:**

- Quiet repetition: stable hours, fixed sources, low drama.
- Structured sprints: intense blocks followed by deliberate recovery.
- Deadline command: clear tests and public dates sharpen me.
- Human accountability: I execute better when someone expects evidence.
- Protected solitude: deep work improves when messages and comparison disappear.
- Energy-aware adaptation: I need different tasks for high- and low-energy windows.
- I keep borrowing rhythms from others and have not found my own.

**Purpose:** Provides direct evidence for the operating profile and its scheduling rules.

## Derived Report Contract

### Cognitive Archetype

Derived from the complete cross-level pattern, led by cognitive clarity, execution friction, resource chaos, performance behavior, consistency, and recovery. No new self-label card is needed.

### Strengths And Vulnerabilities

Derived from repeated evidence across levels. A trait should not be declared from one answer when the rest of the instrument contradicts it. Premium reports should name productive strengths, shadow costs, and triggering conditions.

### Emotional Vault

Use `L6-08` as the primary trigger and corroborate it with identity fusion, external pressure, recovery behavior, comparison response, and family environment. Phrase it as a pattern, not a diagnosis.

### Anchor Vault

Compose supported anchor objects from:

- relationship category
- emotional role
- character category
- purpose
- recovery response
- physical/emotional effect of recalling the anchor

For example, "parental sacrifice anchor" is valid only when the relationship and role selections support it. Never fabricate a person's name.

### Targets

Store and display target post family, rank band, and score orientation separately. Do not convert qualitative options into invented exact numbers.

### Personal Rules And Laws

`L8-06` supplies one explicit law. Additional laws may be derived from repeated behavioral evidence, with each law tied to a vulnerability and a practical enforcement condition.

### Operating Profile

Combine operating rhythm with employment, available study hours, consistency, execution friction, resource pattern, emotional recovery, and accountability signals. The result should explain:

- how the aspirant starts work
- what sustains execution
- what disrupts it
- how recovery should work
- which planning style fits
- which environment should be protected

## Data And Compatibility

Introduce new depth values `free40` and `paid60` while retaining support for `free30` and `paid50` on historical profiles and reports.

- Existing reports remain renderable under their original depth.
- New diagnosis sessions use only `free40` or `paid60`.
- Storage keys must include the depth so old partial sessions do not corrupt new sessions.
- Database constraints must accept all four diagnosis depths during migration.
- Report generation must resolve text for all 60 multiple-choice answers.
- Paid access continues to gate the premium instrument server-side.

## Presentation Constraints

- Preserve the current card component, spacing, motion, typography, answer controls, and progress behavior.
- Do not add decorative card variants or visually mark emotionally sensitive cards as alarming.
- Update only card counts, level totals, access labels, and progress math.
- Keep answer options scannable on mobile. Long labels may wrap but must not alter control width.
- Do not expose hidden dimensions, weights, flags, or scoring language to the aspirant.
